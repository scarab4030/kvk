Combat Manual

Standard Operating Procedures // Scarab Intelligence Office

Scenario 01: Rallying While Dominating Field

ENEMY FLAG
RALLY
Offensive Interceptor Ball

Contains 70% of the active force. Positioned past the rally lead to delete enemy reinforcements before they can reach the target. Constant movement is required to avoid focus fire.

Refill Buffer Zone

Contains 30% of the force. This is the staging area for troops waiting to refill the rally. Players here stay safe while ensuring the rally never drops in troop count.

Field Denial

The primary goal is denial. If the enemy cannot reinforce their structure, the rally burns through health exponentially faster. Do not chase individual kills away from the ball.

Zero-Delay Refills

Marches from the buffer zone must time their arrival so that as one troop batch dies in the rally, another arrives immediately. This maintains maximum damage per second (DPS).

The Hard Rule

If the field is lost, the rally is lost. Priority 1 is always holding the field in Ball 1. Do not leave the interceptor zone unless ordered to reset.

Scenario 02: Garrison - Defending During UpTime

OUR FLAG
ENEMY RALLY
COUNTER
Enemy Aggressor

Track the rage bar. High rage = incoming AoE skill. Filling the garrison during peak rage leads to catastrophic troop losses due to getting caught in AOE. Wait for the cooldown window to reinforce.

Counter Rally -Neutralizer

Launched at the enemy's rally to counter it and make it weak. This forces their field support to split focus. A successful counter-rally often breaks the enemy's ability to swarm our flag.

Rage Syncing

Officers will call for "Dumping." This means sending full marches to the garrison only when the enemy leader's rage bar is empty. Efficiency is life.

Ball Tightness

The Defensive Ball must stay within the alliance territory. This allows us to utilise allaince buffs to affect all defending marches, increasing overall survivability.

Counter Pressure

If the counter-rally lands, the enemy swarm usually retreats. Use this window to heal and swap out low-health marches in the garrison.

ENEMY RALLY
Hopping Relay (Arrows)

Shows city-teleportation paths. Tier 2 cities wait for a front-line gap to open, then hop in to maintain 100% proximity to the flag. This ensures zero travel time for troops.

Front-Line Cluster

A circle of protection. These cities act as the primary ports for reinforcement. They are shielded by the swarm and the flag's own defensive attributes.

Enemy Hazard Zone

The area controlled by the enemy. Any troop march sent from far away will be intercepted here. City-hopping to the left side eliminates this danger entirely.

Leapfrog Strategy

When a Tier 1 city is out of troops or bubbles, the Tier 2 city on the far left instantly takes its spot. This "Leapfrogging" keeps our reinforcement lines invisible and immune to enemy intercept balls.

Stall Victory

Downtime isn't about killing the enemy; it's about not dying. By relaying fresh cities and troops, we drain the enemy's speedups while we only use heal-clicks. We win by outlasting their patience.
